USA Music × Political Polarization

Decade-level comparison of Billboard Hot 100 dynamics and congressional polarization (1958–2024).

Visualization

Music metrics are computed from Billboard Hot 100 weekly charts (1958–2024). Political polarization is computed from Voteview DW-NOMINATE party means. The 1950s include only 1958–1959; the 2020s include 2020–mid 2024. This visualization highlights correlations and temporal convergence rather than claiming direct causality.

Method (short)

This project compares decade-level music chart dynamics with a standardized measure of U.S. political polarization. Music metrics are aggregated from weekly Hot 100 rankings, while polarization is derived from party mean ideology scores (DW-NOMINATE). The visualization supports exploratory analysis and does not claim direct causality.

Important limitations

The Hot 100 starts in 1958, so the 1950s decade is partial (1958–1959). The 2020s decade is also partial (2020–mid 2024), which may bias longevity metrics downward due to right-censoring.
